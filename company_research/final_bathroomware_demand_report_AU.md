# Australia bathroomware demand analysis (online + search + professional specification proxies)

**Capture date:** 2026-02-09

## Data sources used (publicly accessible)

- Online retail demand proxies: Bunnings (review counts on category listings); Amazon AU Best Sellers category ranks (via `ref=zg_bs` links); The Blue Space editorial "Most Popular Toilets" models.

- Search demand: Google Trends AU (past 12 months) related queries for representative category terms (toilet suite, basin mixer, bathroom vanity, freestanding bath, bathroom basin, floor waste).

- Professional specification proxies: manufacturer specifier portals (e.g., Specify by Caroma) + specifier product libraries (ArchiPro AU) + linked spec-sheet PDFs (e.g., Stormtech, Methven, Fienza).

## Primary analysis: highest-demand bathroomware in online channels (Australia)

- Bunnings uses _review count_ as a proxy for high-volume/popular items on its listings.
- Amazon AU uses _Best Sellers list position_ (lower rank = higher sales velocity in that category).
- The Blue Space list is editorially curated (model-level) and used as an additional popularity signal for toilets.

## Comparison: online consumer demand vs professional specification proxies

- **Toilets:** Consumer-facing popularity signals (Blue Space) and specifier proxies both emphasize Caroma Luna/Urbane II lines and rimless (Cleanflush) hygiene + WELS 4-star dual flush.

- **Tapware:** Online demand includes a long tail (including Amazon import brands), while professional proxies concentrate on WaterMark/WELS/lead-free compliance and coordinated collections (e.g., Caroma Liano II / Dorf lines / Methven).

- **Drainage:** Online signals skew to small parts (grates/wastes) with reviews, whereas specifier proxies highlight linear/channel drains with WaterMark/AS3500 compliance (e.g., Stormtech).

- **Vanities/basins:** Online marketplace categories can mix storage cabinets with true vanities; specifier proxies separate vanity cabinets and basin-tops with dimensional and plumbing-interface requirements (overflow wastes, sizes).
